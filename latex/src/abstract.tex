\begin{abstract}
    The Domain Name System (DNS) is used every day by end users to resolve
    domain names to IP addresses. While the encryption of these queries to hide
    browsing habits from third parties is gaining adoption, it still fails to
    address the privacy issue of sending your identity and destination to a
    single DNS resolver operator. Solutions to this problem by anonymizing the
    client have been proposed, but they fall short in dimensions such as
    performance, threat model, and deployability. While performance regarding
    latency is important, the other dimensions should also be considered when
    comparing approaches. In this study, we reevaluate DNS-over-HTTPS-over-Tor
    (DoHoT) and compare it against other proposed solutions, taking into
    consideration additional aspects such as threat model and deployability.
    Additionally, we explore various circuit configurations through the Tor
    network to enhance query latency when utilizing Tor-based anonymization
    approaches. We could decrease the latency of DNS queries while preserving
    the privacy guarantees in our threat model for anonymous resolution. We
    compare the best-performing configuration of DoHoT against other
    approaches. Although the reduced latency is behind that of the other
    solutions, DoHoT is ready for use today with no additional infrastructure
    required, alongside a much stronger threat model.

    \keywords{DNS \and Oblivious DNS \and Tor \and Encryption \and Proxy \and Latency}
\end{abstract}
