\begin{abstract}
    The Domain Name System (DNS) is used everyday by end users to resolve
    domain names to IP addresses. While the encryption of these queries to hide
    browsing habits from third-parties are picking up adoption, it still fails
    to address the privacy problem of sending who you are and where you are
    going to a single DNS resolver provider. Multiple solutions to this problem
    by anonymizing the client have been proposed but there is either a lack of
    deployment or too costly in regards to latency. This study aims to
    investigate potential performance increases in the DNS-over-HTTPS-over-Tor
    (DoHoT) approach. By exploring different circuit configurations of the Tor
    network we found that we could decrease the latency of DNS queries while
    preserving the privacy guarantees in our threat model for anonymous
    resolution. We compare the best performing configuration of DoHoT against
    other approaches and even though the reduced latency is behind the other
    solutions, DoHoT is ready to use today with no additional infrastructure
    required. Our findings demonstrate that, under realistic assumptions, a
    lightweight Tor-based approach to anonymous resolution offers a compelling
    balance between privacy and performance.
    \keywords{DNS \and Oblivious DNS \and Tor \and Encryption \and Proxy \and Latency}
\end{abstract}
