\section{Conclusion} \label{sec:conclusion}

In this study, we explored using the Tor network to anonymize \ac{dns} queries
through the \ac{dohot} approach. Our measurements show that \ac{dohot}
performance can be significantly improved by reducing circuit length to two
hops and by constraining relay selection to localized geographic regions. These
optimizations balance preserving query anonymity in our threat model and
achieving acceptable query latency, making \ac{dohot} a viable option for
privacy-conscious users.

Compared to existing privacy-preserving \ac{dns} protocols like \ac{odns} and
\ac{odoh}, \ac{dohot} benefits from using a well-established anonymity network
without requiring new infrastructure or trust assumptions. However, it inherits
limitations from Tor itself, including potential scalability issues and
susceptibility to censorship. Ultimately, our findings highlight the potential
of Tor-based \ac{dns} anonymization with tailored circuit designs to offer a
practical and privacy-respecting solution for everyday users.
